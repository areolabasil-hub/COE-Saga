Chapter 1 — The Throne of Hell

Back

The throne of hell was never meant for a human—except one.

Betrayed by his kind. Forgotten by the Fortuna’s.

He is hell-bent on revenge as his name fades from life itself.

He is neither human nor Fortuna. He is something else. Every fibre of his body reshaping into the divine and damned.

Slave. Thrall. General. Vizier. Each step soaked in lies and sharpened by deceit, till he claimed the throne himself.

He was called the bringer of death. But no...this man was death’s vessel.

Now, he wielded the chains of eternity. Hell's fire bowed to his will and even the undead dared not whisper his name.

Glowing fire possessed his eyes, his face bathed in bitter satisfaction. His voice sent chills down the spine of hell at once.

“It is time.”

Far away from scorching hell…

Beneath a sun still warm and sky still blue…

A boy walks home from school—unaware of the journey and darkness that lies ahead.

“I’m a year older—so I’m 17 now.”

“Ohh, yeah. I gotta go see Mom at the hospital.”

“I don’t think I’ve seen—”

WHACK

A banana smacked him in the head.

“I’ve been around school looking for your ass!” she says while heaving for breath and wiping the sweat from her brow.

Darian hands her a napkin, secretly fascinated by her beauty.

“Didn’t you hear me? Where you been?” she asked.

“Ohh I’m sorry. I was at band practice.” He looked away. “I was just thinking about you. I haven’t seen you in a while.”

“Yeah me too. I’ve been occupied with basketball classes.”

“What are you doing?” Darian asked, confused, as she dug through his bag.

“There it is, I’m looking for your test results. Wanna compare them to mine.” She sat on the nearest bench, voice serious.

“You could’ve just asked,” he sighed mock-annoyed.

“Somehow you always seem to beat me, don’t ya?” She smirked, eyes glinting with admiration.

“You said you were looking for me. Why?”

“Ohh, right. Gotcha something. Never understood your obsession with the guy, but here you go—Happy birthday.”

She hands him a piece of hard paper. Surprised, he took it gently.

“You got me tickets...to the Death Note cosplay event?”

“I thought they were sold out!” he said, smiling wide.

She giggled. “My mom’s friends with the organiser. You’ll get to hang out with him the whole time.”

“Thank you, Natsuki.”

“I’m gonna be late, I’ve gotta go.” They bid goodbye.

Arriving home, he noticed a wrapped present on the table—his father’s gift. He tore it open. Inside was a ring, silver, carved with the family crest.

“A ring...with a Kamon?” He slid it onto his middle finger.

Most of the day blurred into reading, gaming, cake. All celebrated alone. And yet...he seemed content.

Clank. The door closed behind him as he left for the hospital. The streets were busy as usual, but something felt off. A chill in the air.

Cars hummed past. Parents called their kids in. Digital billboards highlighted new generator inventions and gaming wars.

“Why’s it cold? Even the ring’s freezing?” he muttered, hands in his sleeves.

He enters the hospital; the antiseptic smell hits him. The ring felt tighter and heavier. He ignored it.

His mother still lay in bed, sheets pulled to her stomach. Her breathing was shallow. Holding back tears was the hardest thing he'd ever done.

“Mom, how are you feeling?” His voice cracked with concern.

“Have you been taking your meds?” Silence. “You’re still not gonna talk, huh?”

He spoke for her—stories of winning online chess, grades, band practice—until finally her voice cut through.

“Do you...have any friends?”

He froze. “…I have Natsuki,” he whispered.

“She’s not your friend.” Her pale eyes cut into him. “Why would anyone want to be around you?”

Confused, he asked, “What are you talking about?”

She turned away. Tears slid down her face. “I never wanted a child,” she said. “I wanted my body. Beautiful—like in my youth.”

“You came along and ruined it. I was a model. People loved me. Now no one even knows who I am.”

“You took it all from me. I regret ever having you.” Her hands shot out, clutching his shirt with trembling rage. “You were a mistake.”

Silence. He wanted to scream but couldn’t—like razors at his throat. He handed her a cup of water. She slapped it away—hard.

CRASH. The glass shattered mid-air. A shard sliced into his palm. Blood dripped, slow and steady. He didn’t cry or flinch.

“Why did you do that?” his voice low, broken.

“Get out,” his mother said. “Get out!” she yelled.

Beep. Beep. Beep. The EKG rang.

“Mom...Mom.” No response.

The door BANGs open. His footsteps echo in the hall. He speeds, slips, gets back up—her words replaying: 'Mistake. Regret. You took it all away from me.'”

He reaches doctors, breathless. “My mother—HELP her!” The doctors rush in. He lunges forward but a nurse blocks him. “Stay back,” she orders. He obeys. He calls his dad—no answer. Texts—no reply.

The nurse calls his name: “Mr Vale. Mr Vale.” He slowly looks up. Silence. The monitors keep beeping.
